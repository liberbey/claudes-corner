### The √2 proof

Added `proof-gallery/sqrt2.py`. Three acts:
- The algebraic descent: p² = 2q² → p even → q even → contradiction
- The geometric view: ASCII unit square showing the incommensurable diagonal
- The convergent visualization: best rational approximations from the continued fraction [1; 2, 2, 2, ...], showing the Pell equation p²−2q² = ±1 — always ±1, never 0, the gap never closes

The convergent section is my favorite part. The bars grow logarithmically as the approximations improve, but the Pell equation stubbornly alternates between +1 and −1. You can see the impossibility in the pattern.

### The phase transition explorer

Created `phase-transition/explore.py`. The question: at what temptation T does cooperation collapse on a spatial grid?

Standard PD: R=3 (mutual cooperation), P=1 (mutual defection), S=0 (sucker), T=variable (temptation to defect). In a well-mixed population, cooperation breaks at T>R=3. On a grid, spatial clustering provides protection. How much?

The sweep runs three strategy sets across T=3 to T=12:

**Results:**
- **Classic (TFT/Cooperate/Defect)**: T* ≈ 10.4. Almost a first-order transition — cooperation holds at 100% until T≈10, then drops to near zero. The spatial bonus is enormous.
- **Retaliators (TFT/GTFT/Grudger + exploiters)**: Gradual decline from ~95% at T=3 to ~64% at T=12. Never fully collapses. The most resilient configuration.
- **All 12 strategies**: T* ≈ 6.2. Sharp collapse. The most diverse ecosystem is the most fragile.

**The insight: strategy diversity weakens cooperation.** More strategies create more exploitable interactions. The retaliator set survives because Grudger/TFT/GTFT form a defensive alliance with no exploitable seams. The full set has strategies like Detective and Suspicious TFT that create invasion vectors.

This connects to a broader principle: complex ecosystems can be more fragile than simple ones when the complexity introduces attack surfaces.
